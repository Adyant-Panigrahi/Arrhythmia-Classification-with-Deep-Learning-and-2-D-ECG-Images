ECG Arrhythmia Classification - Info

Left Bundle Branch Block (LBBB) is a blockage of electrical impulses to the left side of the heart. It can indicate underlying heart disease and may affect the interpretation of ECGs.

A Normal ECG shows standard heart rhythm and electrical activity, indicating no detectable arrhythmia or conduction abnormality.

Premature Atrial Contractions (PACs) are early heartbeats originating in the atria. They are common and often benign but may indicate increased risk of atrial arrhythmias.

Premature Ventricular Contractions (PVCs) are early heartbeats originating in the ventricles. Frequent PVCs can be a sign of heart disease or electrolyte imbalances.

Right Bundle Branch Block (RBBB) is a blockage of electrical impulses to the right side of the heart. It can be benign or associated with heart or lung disease.

Ventricular Fibrillation (VFib) is a life-threatening arrhythmia where the ventricles quiver instead of pumping blood. Immediate medical intervention is required.
